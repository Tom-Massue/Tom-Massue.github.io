About Me

I'm a PhD candidate working on environmental DNA (eDNA) as a tool to monitor freshwater biodiversity. My research focuses on EPT taxa — Ephemeroptera, Plecoptera and Trichoptera — classic bioindicators of stream water quality, sampled across rivers in French Guiana.

Concretely, my work covers the full chain: 16S metabarcoding of short Illumina amplicons (~215 bp), and the bioinformatic processing of that data through pipelines such as FROGS (SWARM and DADA2 clustering) run on the Genotoul HPC cluster, followed by taxonomic affiliation (RDP, BLCA, LCA) and statistical analysis with metabaR.

I care about reproducible, well-justified methodology — most of my pipeline choices are backed by published validation work (e.g. Wang et al. 2007 for the RDP classifier, Bacci et al. 2015 for threshold validation on short amplicons).

Research Projects

Mon travail de thèse étudie comment l'ADN environnemental (eDNA) présent dans l'eau des rivières permet de détecter et suivre les communautés d'insectes aquatiques EPT (Ephemeroptera, Plecoptera, Trichoptera), des bioindicateurs classiques de la qualité de l'eau, en Guyane française.

Concrètement : metabarcoding 16S sur amplicons courts (~215 pb, Illumina paired-end), puis traitement bioinformatique complet — clustering, affiliation taxonomique, statistiques — exécuté sur le cluster HPC Genotoul.
